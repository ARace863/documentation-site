### 2. General Procedures

#### 2.1 General provisions

**Bahrain Approach (APP)** provides surveillance-based radar control services to aircraft departing, arriving, or transiting the **Bahrain Terminal Maneuvering Area (TMA)**. Services are provided to:

* Aircraft released from the ACC Controller descending into the vertical and lateral limits of the TMA.
* Aircraft transiting the TMA until clear of vertical and/or lateral limits.
* Departing aircraft from covered aerodromes until outside the TMA limits and transferred to the next relevant controller.
* Aircraft coming from uncontrolled airspace into the TMA.

#### 2.2 Separation minima

###### 2.2.1 Surveillance-based separation

All aircraft within the **Bahrain Terminal Maneuvering Area (TMA)** must be separated with a minimum of **5 NM** longitudinal separation. In the event that longitudinal separation cannot be achieved, a minimum vertical separation of **1,000 ft** must be applied.

Speed control may be issued to maintain separation, especially for climbing traffic or aircraft completing turns within laterally confined airspace.

###### 2.2.2 Wake turbulence separation

The following Wake Turbulence Separation (WTS) shall be applied to aircraft being provided with Surveillance Based Separation (SBS). The **5 NM** longitudinal separation requirements within the TMA **override any wake turbulence minima lower than the minimum longitudinal separation requirements.**

| **Trailing Aircraft** | **Behind** | **Leading Aircraft** | **Distance Separation Minima** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Light (L) |  | Medium (M) | 5 NM |
| Medium (M) |  | Heavy (H) | 5 NM |
| Light (L) |  | Heavy (H) | 6 NM |
| Heavy (H) |  | Heavy (H) | 4 NM |
| Medium (M) |  | A380 | 7 NM |
| Light (L) |  | A380 | 8 NM |

#### 2.3 Aerodromes within the TMA

The Approach Controller is responsible for **Bahrain International Airport (OBBI)**, **Isa Airbase (OBBS)**, and **Sakhir Air base (OBKH)**. Note that both Isa Air Base and Sakhir Air Base are military airfields to which ATC services are not provided on the ground.

#### 2.4 Restricted Airspace

The Bahrain TMA contains 3 restricted sections of airspace that must not be entered at any time unless approved by the Approach Controller for operations in/out of Isa Airbase or Sakhir Air Base.

| Restricted Airspace Identifier | Vertical Limits | Validity |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **R57** | GND - UNLTD | Permanent |
| **P42** | GND - UNLTD | Permanent |
| **R66** | GND - UNLTD | Permanent |

### 3. Areas of Responsibility

#### 3.1 General

The Approach Controller is responsible for the airspace surrounding Bahrain Airport within a **40 NM radius** centred on the **BHR VHF Omnidirectional Range (VOR)**. Controllers must maintain a plan of action at all times to sequence traffic and avoid a loss of separation.

#### 3.2 Surrounding Airspace

The Bahrain TMA is surrounded by Saudi Arabia to the West and Qatar to the South. Controllers must work closely with adjacent controlling units to ensure safe and efficient operations.

## 4. Transfer Procedures

### 4.1 General

Due to the location of Bahrain and the direction of some flights, not all aircraft will be transferred to the en-route controller as they will not enter their airspace. The Approach Controller must be aware of the appropriate controller to transfer an aircraft to based on lateral/vertical limits and adjacent control units.

### 4.2 Tag Management

The Approach Controller must ensure aircraft tags are managed correctly and promptly upon issuing instructions. This ensures all surrounding controlling units are aware of planned intentions. Altitudes, flight levels, assigned headings, directs, and assigned speeds must be updated in the tag accordingly.

!!! note "Letter of Agreement (LOAs)"
Please ensure you have read and understood all Letter of Agreements before controlling on the Network.

## 5. Arrivals

### 5.1 STAR, Approach, and Runway assignment

#### 5.1.1 Initial contact

Aircraft arriving into the Bahrain TMA must be provided with instructions to fly assigned routings, descend separated from other traffic, and remain under speed control. In the absence of an online ACC Controller, the Approach Controller should provide this on initial contact.

!!! example
**Controller**: "DHX268, Identified, Cleared ORDIG1 Arrival, ILS Approach Runway 30R, descend altitude 8000 ft, QNH 1010."

If the ACC Controller is online, they will advise the aircraft to "expect" a particular STAR. The Approach Controller remains responsible for clearing the aircraft onto the STAR.

!!! note "Identifying Aircraft"
Ensure all aircraft are identified on initial contact. Aircraft should be assigned an individual squawk code if not already and squawking mode 'Charlie' (altitude information).

#### 5.1.2 STAR Assignment

Bahrain offers 3 different RNAV (Area Navigation) Standard Terminal Arrival Routes (STAR). All RNAV arrivals into Bahrain are RNAV1. If an aircraft is unequipped or experiencing GPS issues, they must be issued vectors and the STAR cancelled.

| Procedure | Originating Traffic | Suitable Runways |
| --- | --- | --- |
| KOBOK1 | North West | RWY12L/30R |
| LADNA1 | West | RWY12L/30R |
| ORDIG1 | North East | RWY12L/30R |

#### 5.1.3 Instrument Approach

Both RWY12L and RWY30R offer three types of approaches:

* **Instrument Landing System (ILS)** (Precision)
* **VHF Omnidirectional Range (VOR)** (Non-Precision)
* **Required Navigation Performance (RNP)** (Non-Precision)

The ILS approach is the standard. If requested by the pilot or the ILS is unserviceable, non-precision approaches may be approved, traffic permitting.

!!! note "GPS/RNP approaches"
Despite providing vertical guidance, RNP approaches are deemed non-precision due to their reliance on satellites.

#### 5.1.4 Visual approach

Visual Meteorological Conditions (VMC) must exist (ceiling > 1500 ft, ground visibility > 5 km). The controller cannot issue a visual approach clearance without the pilot requesting it and confirming the airfield is in sight.

!!! example
**Controller**: "GFA771, report airfield in sight."
**Pilot**: "Airfield in sight, GFA771."
**Controller**: "GFA771, cleared visual approach RWY30R, QNH 1003."

### 5.3 Holding

If the controller is unable to manage the traffic load, aircraft must be placed into the hold until they can be sequenced. Within the Bahrain TMA, there are 6 published holding procedures:

| Hold Identifier | Vertical Limits | Inbound Course | Max Speed | Turn Direction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| OBSAS | 3,000 ft - 13,000 ft | 207° | 240 kts | Right |
| ALRAS | 3,000 ft - 13,000 ft | 300° | 210 kts | Right |
| NAGAT | 3,000 ft - 13,000 ft | 120° | 210 kts | Left |
| MARWA | 5,000 ft - FL 150 | 302° | 240 kts | Left |
| RAZAN | 5,000 ft - FL 150 | 122° | 240 kts | Right |
| BHR (VOR) | 25,000 ft - FL 170 | *Various* | *Various* | *Various* |

#### 5.3.1 Holding Instructions and Altitudes

Aircraft must remain vertically separated by 1,000 ft in the hold. The controller must ensure each aircraft has its own block altitude (e.g., 1st aircraft at 8,000 ft, 2nd at 9,000 ft).

!!! example
**Controller**: "GFA164, enter published hold at OBSAS, descend altitude 8000 ft, speed 230 kts, expect 10 minute delay."

If an aircraft does not have the hold in their FMC, provide the full procedure:

!!! example
**Controller**: "GFA164, enter hold at OBSAS, inbound holding course 207°, descend altitude 8000 ft, speed 230 kts, right hand turns, you can expect 10 minute delay."

To cancel a hold:

* **Via STAR:** `"GFA164, on completion of this pattern, cancel the hold, descend altitude 4000 ft via the STAR, speed 210 kts"`
* **Via Vectors:** `"GFA164, cancel hold, turn right heading 120°, descend altitude 4000 ft, speed 210 kts"`

### 5.4 Final Approach Sequencing

#### 5.4.1 Vectoring for ILS Approaches

Aircraft should be given an intercepting vector within 30 degrees of the final approach course. The absolute maximum intercept vector is 40 degrees.

!!! example
**Controller**: "DHX268, turn right heading 270 degrees, descend altitude 1500 ft, cleared ILS Approach RWY30R, report established."

#### 5.4.2 Clearing Aircraft for RNP Approaches

Aircraft flying an RNP approach will be cleared via the Initial Approach Fix (IAF) or Intermediate Fix (IF).

!!! example
**Controller**: "DHX268, proceed direct LOVAL, descend altitude 2500 ft, via LOVAL, cleared RNP Approach RWY12L, report established on the final approach track, QNH 1003."

!!! note "Differences"
As the RNP is not a precision approach, aircraft should be told to "report final" instead of "report established".

#### 5.4.3 Clearing Aircraft for the VOR Approach

Aircraft can fly the full procedural (overflying the BHR VOR and performing the tear-drop procedure) or be provided with vectors to intercept the inbound radial.

!!! example
**Controller** (Procedural): "GFA013, via the BHR VOR, cleared procedural VOR/DME approach RWY12L, report on outbound radial, descend altitude 2500 ft, QNH 1003."
**Controller** (Vectors): "GFA013, turn right heading 150 degrees, descend altitude 1500 ft, cleared VOR approach RWY12L, report established on the inbound radial, QNH 1003."

!!! warning "Traffic Load"
The Approach Controller must not accept non-standard approach requests if the traffic situation in the TMA does not allow.

## 6. Departures

### 6.1 General Departure Procedures

Bahrain Approach is responsible for ensuring all aircraft departing the TMA are safely separated vertically and/or laterally before transferring to the next controller. Due to the lack of Standard Instrument Departures (SIDs) out of Bahrain, controllers must maintain a full understanding of the airspace structure to transition aircraft into the en route environment safely.

#### 6.1.1 Initial Contact

Upon initial contact, departing aircraft transferred from TWR must report their Callsign, Heading, and Passing Altitude.

!!! example
**Controller**: "GFA505, Report passing altitude."
**Pilot**: "Passing 2500 ft, GFA505."
**Controller**: "GFA505, Identified on departure, continue climb FL170, route direct MEDMA."

If an aircraft does not report passing altitude, it must be requested to verify radar contact (should be within +/- 300 ft of radar display).

### 6.3 Lateral Profile

Aircraft will initially contact the Approach Controller on an assigned heading from the DLV Controller. The Approach Controller will either provide additional vectors toward the airway or a direct to a fix.

Delivery of direct instructions varies depending on the aircraft's current lateral profile:

!!! example
**Aircraft flying an Assigned Heading:**
**Controller**: "GFA071, track direct SODAK."

```
**Aircraft flying filed routing:**
**Controller**: "GFA071, route direct SODAK."
```
